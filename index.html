IVF Process Step by Step

1. Ovarian Stimulation

A woman seeking to conceive undergoes fertility treatment, receiving various medications in the form of tablets and injections. The process includes continuous monitoring of follicle development through ultrasonography.

2. Egg Retrieval

Follicle retrieval, performed under ultrasound guidance, involves screening follicular fluid for eggs in a day care procedure that requires anesthesia.

3. Sperm collection

The partner is requested to provide semen through masturbation. In cases of severe male factor infertility or zero sperm count, bloodless surgical techniques can be employed for sperm retrieval. Alternatively, donor sperm is considered when using one's own gametes is not feasible.

4. Fertilization

Egs and sperm are combined in the laboratory to facilitate fertilization.

5. Embryo Culture

The sperm-injected oocyte is cultured in sophisticated incubators for 5-6 days to develop into an embryo.

6. Embryo Transfer

A highly skilled yet simple technique involves transferring one or more embryos into the uterus using an embryo transfer catheter under ultrasound guidance. Typically, this procedure does not require anesthesia.

7. Hormonal Suppport

Luteal support is provided through estrogen and progesterone tablets or injections to aid embryo implantation.

8. Pregnancy Test

A blood test is performed to determine if pregnancy has occurred.

+ What is In Vitro Fertilization(IVF)?

A process of fertilizing an egg with sperm from outside the woman's body. The egg is then placed back inside her uterus to develop.

+ Why opt for In-Vitro Fertilization(IVF)?

One of the major reasons why couples choose IVF is due to infertility issues. For women, these issues may include problems with the uterus or fallopian tubes, poor egg quality, and improper ovulation. Similarly, men may face challenges such as low sperm counts and low sperm motility.

+ Is In Vitro Fertilization Expensive?

Similar to other complex medical treatments, IVF involves highly skilled professionals, advanced laboratories, and equipment. Sometimes, multiple cycles may be necessary to achieve success. Therefore, it can be a significant investment.

+ Does In Vitro Fertilization Work?

Absolutely from 1985 to 2017, there were 1,204,594 reported births resulting from Assisted Reproductive Technology procedures, including IVF. This demonstrates the effectiveness of IVF in helping couples achieve their dream of parenthood.
